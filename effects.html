Effects

What happens if you get sucked into a black hole?

Spaghettification

The process where strong tidal forces from a massive object, like a black hole, stretch an object vertically while compressing it horizontally, causing it to break apart into a long, thin shape resembling spaghetti.

No.2 Tidal Forces

If Earth gets too close, the black hole's immense gravity would stretch and tear the planet apart, a phenomenon known as "spaghettification".

No.3 Accretion Disk

A black hole is often surrounded by a disc of superheated gas and dust that falls into it. This accretion disk can emit dangerous levels of radiation and heat, requiring a significant buffer zone for safety

No.4 Gravitational influence

A black hole's gravitational pull can disrupt the orbits of planets and comets. To be safe, the black hole must be far enough away that it doesn't alter the stable orbits within our solar system
